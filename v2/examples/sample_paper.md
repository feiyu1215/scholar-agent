# National Innovation Demonstration Zones and Regional Entrepreneurial Activity: A Quasi-Experimental Study

## Abstract

This groundbreaking study delves into the transformative potential of National Innovation Demonstration Zones (NIDZs) on regional entrepreneurial activity in China. Using a staggered difference-in-differences design with prefecture-level panel data from 2006 to 2020, we find that NIDZs seem to suggest positive effects on entrepreneurship. Furthermore, it is crucial to note that the magnitude varies across regions. Moreover, one must consider that human capital plays a mediating role. Our findings have important policy implications for innovation-driven development strategies.

## 1. Introduction

Innovation zones have become increasingly important. This paper is the first to systematically test conditional heterogeneity in NIDZ effects on entrepreneurial activity. The literature on innovation zones show that place-based policies can stimulate economic growth (Aghion et al 2019). Furthermore, evidence suggests that the relationship between innovation policy and entrepreneurship is complex (Djankov et al., 2002).

We contribute to the literature in three ways. First, we provide causal evidence. Second, we identify mechanisms. Third, we explore heterogeneity. Additionally, our analysis employs state-of-the-art econometric techniques that ensure the robustness of our findings.

## 2. Literature Review

The existing body of knowledge on place-based innovation policies is extensive and multifaceted. It is widely acknowledged that NIDZs represent a significant policy experiment (Wang & Li, 2018). Furthermore, the extant literature demonstrates that regional innovation systems play a crucial role in economic development (Lundvall, 1992; Nelson, 1993). Moreover, scholars have increasingly recognized the importance of entrepreneurship as a driver of economic growth (Acs et al., 2009).

## 3. Methodology

We employ a staggered difference-in-differences design to identify the causal effect of NIDZs on entrepreneurial activity. The baseline specification is:

EA_it = α + β·NIDZ_it + X_it·γ + μ_i + λ_t + ε_it

where EA is the entrepreneurial activity index (constructed), NIDZ is the treatment indicator, X is a vector of controls, and μ_i and λ_t are prefecture and year fixed effects.

## 4. Results

The empirical results seem to suggest that National Innovation Demonstration Zones might potentially have some impact on regional entrepreneurial activity. The coefficient on NIDZ is significant at the 1% level in our baseline specification (β = 0.32, p < 0.01). Neighboring regions show increased entrepreneurial activity, suggesting spatial spillover effects. The results are marginally significant when using alternative specifications.

## 5. Conclusion

This paper found that NIDZs increase entrepreneurial activity. The results suggest positive effects. Our findings contribute to the growing literature on innovation policy. Future research should explore additional mechanisms and contexts.
