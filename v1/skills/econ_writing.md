# Economics Paper Writing Guidelines

Loaded on-demand when rewriting economics papers. Synthesized from 50+ guides by Cochrane, McCloskey, Shapiro, Head, Bellemare, Goldin, Glaeser, Kremer.

## Core Principles

1. **Reader First**: Write for busy PhD economists who are NOT in your subfield
2. **Triangular Style**: Most important information FIRST, then details. Never bury the lead.
3. **One Contribution**: Every paper has ONE central novel contribution. State it in one paragraph.
4. **Concrete > Abstract**: Say what you FIND, not what you LOOK for. Give coefficients, magnitudes, facts.
5. **Every Word Counts**: Cut ruthlessly. Most paragraphs have too many sentences.
6. **Active Voice, Present Tense**: "I find that..." not "It was found that..."
7. **Simple > Complex**: "Use" not "utilize". Short, common words.

## Introduction Formula (3-5 pages max)

- Para 1-2: THE HOOK (Y matters / is puzzling / is controversial / is big)
- Para 3: THE RESEARCH QUESTION + main result
- Para 4-6: MAIN RESULTS with magnitudes
- Para 7-9: LITERATURE + VALUE ADDED (story, not bibliography)
- Final: ROADMAP (customized, not generic)

## Phrases to Delete on Sight

- "It should be noted that" → just say it
- "It is easy to show that" → just show it
- "In other words" → say it right the first time
- "It is worth noting" → just say it
- "This paper contributes to the literature by" → say what you find
- "We investigate/examine/explore the relationship" → say what you find

## Conclusion (1 page max)

- Reiterate findings DIFFERENTLY from intro
- Policy implications with rough cost-benefit
- 1-2 concrete future directions
- Do NOT add a "limitations" subsection — it belongs in the body
